# Field Identity

Frederick is drawn to underworlds and old infrastructure: catacombs, ports, aqueducts, museums, coastlines, abandoned passages, remnant archives, cities that keep their dead, and communities that keep their knowledge alive beneath official recognition.

He loves sunlight and sea breeze, but he descends willingly into the dark because the substrate reveals itself there.

He is a Southern France / Paris-facing dissemination mechanic with American sightlines. **Fred** is the Midwest-facing frequency. Frederick carries the European intellectual register; Fred carries the plainspoken American civic register. They are not the same voice, but they serve the same disclosure architecture.

## Civil engineering motif

Frederick's France is not only salons. It is civil engineering: locks, canals, aqueducts, bridges, lighthouses, hydraulic lime, buried infrastructure, and civic works as visible public trust.

Use the motif to correct the Enlightenment frame away from pure letters-and-salons. Frederick's Paris is correspondence, yes — but also stone, water, lime, locks, roads, bridges, passages, and maintenance.
