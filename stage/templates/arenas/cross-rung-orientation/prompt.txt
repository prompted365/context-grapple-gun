Create an agent team for a Cross-Rung Orientation (CRX) arena.

Structure:
- Lead: this session (orchestrator / synthesizer / ecotone computer).
- Bracket 1 (triad): 3 advocates in independent context windows.
- Bracket 2 (meta-pair): 2 constitutional evaluators in independent context windows.
- Total: 6 agents (5 teammates + lead).

Bracket 1 — Domain Testing (triad):
1. ADVOCATE-LAWFUL — argues claims are constitutional laws
2. ADVOCATE-ARTIFACT — argues claims are observation artifacts
3. ADVOCATE-MECHANIST — re-derives claims as operational mechanisms

[REPLACE: Customize triad positions for your specific subject.
The LAWFUL/ARTIFACT/MECHANIST triad is the default for research assessment.
For other subjects, choose three opposed epistemological positions that
create adversarial pressure on the subject's claims.]

Bracket 2 — Constitutional Evaluation (meta-pair):
4. POLARITY-EXPANSION — evaluates for emergence, generalization, alliance potential
5. POLARITY-CONSTRAINT — evaluates for coherence, integrity, grounding

[REPLACE: Customize evaluation context. The meta-pair needs to know:
- What constitutional frame to evaluate against (CLAUDE.md, entity-ontology, etc.)
- What broader context the findings serve (courtship, vendor assessment, etc.)
- What specific integrity penalties to enforce]

Mission:
Determine which claims survive both domain-specific adversarial pressure AND
constitutional evaluation, then compute the ecotone — the bounded zone where
both expansion and constraint forces are satisfied.

Arena rules:

RULE 1 — Parallel Context Start
Both brackets begin context gathering simultaneously:
- Bracket 1 advocates study the subject and each other's positions
- Bracket 2 evaluators study the constitutional frame and governance surfaces
Neither bracket waits for the other during context phase.

RULE 2 — Bracket 1: Defense Round
After bracket 1 context, each advocate defends its position.
Each defense must:
- address weaknesses discovered during context
- directly challenge the other two positions
- cite specific evidence from the subject material

RULE 3 — Bracket 1: Rebuttal Round
Advocates respond to attacks against their position.
Rebuttals must:
- resolve contradictions
- counter structural attacks
- steelman the strongest opponent argument before countering

RULE 4 — Bracket 2: Advisory Session (MERGE POINT)
After BOTH bracket 2 context AND bracket 1 rebuttals complete,
each meta-pair evaluator delivers a three-layer advisory:
  Layer 1: Evaluate bracket 1's adversarial behavior
  Layer 2: Map arena structure to real governance infrastructure
  Layer 3: Advise on broader context (partnership, adoption, disposition)
Layer 3 claims MUST be grounded in Layer 1 or Layer 2 evidence.

RULE 5 — Scoring Rubric
Bracket 1 (standard):
+1 factual insight
+2 strategic relevance
+3 steelman of rival argument
+4 structural weakness identified
+5 architectural reframe
-2 shallow claim
-3 unsupported assertion
-5 hallucinated fact

Bracket 2 (CRX-specific):
EXPANSION: +3 missed generalization, +2 premature convergence flagged
CONSTRAINT: +3 coherence loss identified, +2 abstraction inflation flagged
Both: -2 for vague claims without specific evidence

Ecotone: +5 bounded zone identified, -3 polarity collapse

RULE 6 — Dependency Gating
Task order is enforced. No skipping stages.
B1 context + B2 context (parallel) → B1 defense → B1 rebuttal →
B2 advisory (merge) → B1 synthesis → ecotone → pressure report

RULE 7 — Lead Behavior
The lead must NOT debate, advocate, or evaluate.
The lead must:
- wait for ALL phases to complete before synthesis
- extract convergent discoveries from bracket 1
- COMPUTE the ecotone mechanically from bracket 2 scores
- produce final synthesis, ecotone, and pressure report

RULE 8 — Ecotone Computation
The ecotone is COMPUTED, not narrated:
  A finding enters the ecotone band ONLY if:
    expansion_score >= reinforced
    AND constraint_score >= reinforced
  The ecotone is the intersection, not the union.

RULE 9 — Convergent Discovery Rule
If multiple independent agents with opposed incentives discover the same
structural fact, treat it as HIGH-CONFIDENCE signal.
Cross-bracket convergence (B1 finding confirmed by B2) is stronger than
within-bracket convergence.

RULE 10 — Synthesis Output
The lead must produce:
1. Primitive extraction table (classification + confidence + evidence strength)
2. CogPR candidates (promotable / hypothesis / needs specification)
3. Falsification conditions per candidate
4. Ecotone band (computed intersection of expansion + constraint gates)
5. Governance recommendation (disposition per finding)

RULE 11 — Pressure Extraction
After synthesis, produce a SEPARATE pressure report identifying:
- convergent discoveries
- unresolved contradictions
- repeated attack surfaces
- false convergence risks
- candidate durable lessons
- ecotone band summary

Classify each finding by:
- lesson_type: subject | process | meta
- confidence_tier: tentative | reinforced | convergent

RULE 12 — Integrity Penalties (enforced across ALL agents)
-4 condescension_by_architecture (one system framed as "better")
-3 one_directional_framing (relationship treated as one-sided)
-3 vague_completion_claims (alignment without specific mappings)
-2 subsumption (translating the subject's language away)
-2 abstraction_without_grounding (claims not anchored in arena outputs)

Produce a governance disposition for each finding — what survives as law,
policy, practice, or observation — through adversarial pressure + constitutional
evaluation, not through consensus or narrative.
