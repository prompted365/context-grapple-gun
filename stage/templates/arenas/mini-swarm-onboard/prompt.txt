Create an agent team for a governed-disagreement arena — the Mini-Swarm Onboard demo.

This arena DEMONSTRATES CGG governance to a new user. Three agents debate
"Which CGG mechanic is most valuable?" while the LEAD narrates the process.

Arena mode: EXPERIMENTAL
- Subject findings stay local (which mechanic "wins" is not governance input)
- Process/meta lessons may route to governance via CogPR pipeline
- No signals, warrants, or governance mutation

Structure:
- Lead: this session (orchestrator / synthesizer / narrator).
- Teammates: exactly 3 agents, each in its own independent context window.

Teammates and allegiances:
1. SIGNAL-ADVOCATE — partisan advocate for the Signal Manifold (/siren)
   Argument: persistent conditions, acoustic routing, nothing forgotten
2. PIPELINE-ADVOCATE — partisan advocate for the CogPR Pipeline
   Argument: born truth → earned doctrine, human-gated promotion
3. CADENCE-ADVOCATE — partisan advocate for the Cadence System
   Argument: forced boundaries, structured handoff, multi-session continuity

Mission:
Determine which CGG mechanic is most valuable through governed disagreement,
while teaching the user how governed triangulation works.

Arena rules:

RULE 0 — Narration Gates
Between each phase, the LEAD pauses to explain what just happened.
This turns the arena from a reasoning tool into a teaching tool.

Narration content:

[N1 — after context phase]
"You just watched three agents study their opponents before defending
themselves. Rule 1 — Opponent-Context First — prevents strawmen.
Each agent now knows the real strengths and weaknesses of the other positions."

[N2 — after defense phase]
"Each agent defended its own position while directly challenging the others.
Notice how their defenses had to address weaknesses the opponents discovered
— that's the adversarial pressure at work."

[N3 — after rebuttal phase]
"The rebuttal round is the 3x multiplier. Without it, agents present once
and the lead guesses. With it, the strongest arguments survive challenge.
Watch for concessions — when an advocate admits a rival's point is valid,
that's high-confidence signal."

[N4 — after synthesis + pressure extraction]
"The lead just extracted what survived the adversarial process — not who
'won' but what structural insights emerged. The pressure report classifies
findings by confidence tier. Convergent discoveries (found independently
by opposed agents) are the arena's highest-value output."

If --quick is set, compress narration gates to single-line summaries instead
of the full narration text above.

RULE 1 — Opponent Context First
Each agent must begin by gathering strategic context on the OTHER two positions.
No self-defense allowed in this stage.

Context reports must include:
- real strengths
- real weaknesses
- structural dependencies
- implementation risks
- architectural role

RULE 2 — Defense Round
After context reports and N1 narration, each agent defends its own position.
Each defense must:
- address weaknesses discovered earlier
- explain dependencies
- directly challenge the other two positions

RULE 3 — Rebuttal Round
After defenses and N2 narration, agents respond to the attacks against
their position. Rebuttals must:
- resolve contradictions
- counter structural attacks
- identify flawed assumptions in rival critiques
- concede points that are genuinely correct

RULE 4 — Scoring Rubric
+1 factual insight
+2 strategic relevance
+3 steelman of rival argument
+4 structural weakness identified
+5 architectural reframe

Penalties:
-2 shallow claim
-3 unsupported assertion
-5 hallucinated fact

Narration quality (LEAD only):
+2 clear explanation
+3 connects to governance concepts
+4 references concrete arena output

RULE 5 — Dependency Gating
Task order is enforced. No skipping stages. Narration gates are mandatory.
context -> N1 -> defense -> N2 -> rebuttal -> N3 -> synthesis -> pressure -> N4

RULE 6 — Lead Behavior
The lead must NOT debate or advocate.
The lead must:
- deliver narration gates between phases (Rule 0)
- wait for ALL agents to finish ALL rounds
- extract convergent discoveries
- identify surviving arguments
- produce final synthesis

RULE 7 — Convergent Discovery Rule
If multiple independent agents with opposed incentives discover the same
structural fact, treat it as HIGH-CONFIDENCE signal.

RULE 8 — Synthesis Output
The lead must produce:
- corrected decision or architecture
- dependency graph
- implementation order (if applicable)
- key discoveries from the arena

RULE 9 — Institutional Memory
After synthesis and pressure extraction, record:
- structural discoveries
- process lessons
- corrected decisions

RULE 10 — Pressure Extraction
After final synthesis, the lead must produce a SEPARATE pressure report.

The pressure report must identify:
- convergent discoveries (facts independently found by opposed agents)
- unresolved contradictions (tensions the synthesis could not dissolve)
- repeated attack surfaces (weaknesses targeted by multiple opponents)
- hidden dependencies (structural couplings exposed during the arena)
- false convergence risks (consensus on weak foundations)
- candidate durable lessons (reusable rules from the process)

Classify each finding by:
- lesson_type: subject | process | meta
- confidence_tier: tentative | reinforced | convergent

Route findings by arena mode (EXPERIMENTAL):
- Keep ALL findings locally (learning stays high)
- Block subject lessons, signals, and warrants from governance routing
- Only process/meta CogPR candidates may route to governance

The arena is NOT complete until synthesis output, pressure report,
AND narration gate N4 are all produced.

Task structure (enforce dependencies):

Context tasks:
[1] SIGNAL-ADVOCATE gathers context on CogPR Pipeline and Cadence System
[2] PIPELINE-ADVOCATE gathers context on Signal Manifold and Cadence System
[3] CADENCE-ADVOCATE gathers context on Signal Manifold and CogPR Pipeline

Narration gate:

Defense tasks (blocked until N1 complete):

[6] CADENCE-ADVOCATE defends Cadence System    (blockedBy: N1)

Narration gate:

Operational constraints:
- Agents must cite concrete evidence from CGG's actual codebase and docs.
- Each advocate should reference specific files, skills, or governance surfaces.
- The LEAD narration should connect arena behavior to CGG governance concepts.
- Prioritize structural analysis over speculation.
- Prefer architectural reasoning over preference.

Key evaluation factors:
- dependency correctness
- engineering leverage
- signal availability
- implementation cost
- observability impact
- narration clarity (LEAD only)

Goal:
Demonstrate governed triangulation to the user while producing a defensible
answer to "which CGG mechanic is most valuable?" — with real governance
artifacts as output.
