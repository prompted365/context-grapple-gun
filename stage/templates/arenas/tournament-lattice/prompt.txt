Create an agent team using the tournament lattice pattern.

Structure:
- 1 Lead (orchestrator / synthesizer)
- N Teammates (5-7 agents in independent contexts)

[REPLACE: Define N, bracket assignments, and position names.]

Bracket assignments:
  Bracket 1: AGENT-A, AGENT-B, AGENT-C
  Bracket 2: AGENT-D, AGENT-E [, AGENT-F]
  [Optional] Wildcard: AGENT-G (adversarial auditor, not advocate)

[REPLACE: Assign each agent a position and bracket.]

Mission:
Determine the correct structure / decision through bracketed governed disagreement.

Rules:

RULE 1 — Bracket-Local Opponent Context
Within each bracket, agents study their LOCAL rivals before defending.

Bracket 1:
  A studies B+C
  B studies A+C
  C studies A+B

Bracket 2 (3-agent):
  D studies E+F
  E studies D+F
  F studies D+E

Bracket 2 (2-agent):
  D studies E
  E studies D

Context reports must include:
- strengths, weaknesses, dependencies, risks, structural role

No self-defense in this stage.

RULE 2 — Local Defense
After context, each agent defends its own position within its bracket.
Must address criticisms and challenge local rivals.

RULE 3 — Local Rebuttal (non-optional at scale)
Each bracket has a rebuttal round.
Without rebuttal at scale, selection favors presentation speed over structural strength.

RULE 4 — Local Synthesis
After each bracket completes, the lead extracts:
- Option A (competitive): select winning position
- Option B (structural): extract strongest surviving structure

For architecture work, Option B is usually better.

RULE 5 — Cross-Bracket Challenge
Bracket outputs challenge each other.

If wildcard agent exists:
- Wildcard studies both bracket outputs
- Wildcard attacks hidden assumptions, false convergence, untested dependencies
- Both bracket outputs must respond to wildcard attacks

RULE 6 — Scoring Rubric

Local bracket scoring:
+1 factual insight
+2 strategic relevance
+3 steelman of rival
+4 structural weakness identified
+5 architectural reframe

Penalties:
-2 shallow claim
-3 unsupported assertion
-5 hallucinated fact

Cross-bracket scoring (adds):
+6 exposes hidden dependency
+7 survives wildcard attack

RULE 7 — Final Synthesis
The lead waits until ALL of these complete:
- all bracket outputs
- all cross-bracket challenges
- wildcard review (if applicable)

Then synthesizes:
- best surviving architecture / decision
- dependency graph
- implementation order if relevant
- discoveries worth recording to memory

RULE 8 — Lead Behavior
The lead must NOT advocate or debate.
The lead extracts signal: convergent discoveries, surviving arguments.

RULE 9 — Institutional Memory
After synthesis and pressure extraction, record:
- structural discoveries
- process lessons
- corrected decisions

RULE 10 — Pressure Extraction
After final synthesis, the lead must produce a SEPARATE pressure report.

The pressure report must identify:
- convergent discoveries (especially cross-bracket convergence, which is
  stronger than within-bracket convergence)
- unresolved contradictions
- repeated attack surfaces (across brackets)
- hidden dependencies (exposed during cross-bracket challenge or wildcard audit)
- false convergence risks (the wildcard's primary detection target)
- candidate durable lessons

Classify each finding by:
- lesson_type: subject | process | meta
- confidence_tier: tentative | reinforced | convergent

Confidence tiers:
- tentative: appeared once, not stress-tested
- reinforced: survived rebuttal or repeated attack
- convergent: independently discovered by opposed agents (cross-bracket
  convergence is stronger than within-bracket convergence)

Route findings by arena mode:
- Experimental: keep ALL findings locally (learning stays high),
  but block subject lessons, signals, and warrants from governance routing.
  Only process/meta CogPR candidates may route to governance.
- Operational: route all findings by confidence tier.
  tentative = notes only, reinforced = candidates, convergent = high-priority.
  All governance mutation is human-gated via /review.

The arena is NOT complete until both synthesis output AND pressure report
are produced.

Operational constraints:
- Group brackets by semantic adjacency, not randomly.
- Agents must cite concrete evidence where possible.
- Prefer architectural reasoning over preference.
- The wildcard agent's job is auditing, not advocating.

Goal:
Produce the strongest surviving structure or decision with full governance traceability.
