Surprise Assessment

3 Genuine Surprises

Hot-Potato Relay

Compute admission invariants passed MINE -> CATALYZE -> HARVEST through voluntary releases, ending up where none of the advocates initially placed them. The adversarial process discovered a better owner than any individual position proposed.

NIH Self-Score Enabled Consensus

CATALYZE's honest 5.5/10 NIH self-assessment was the single most consequential move. Without it, the naming collision argument would have blocked all progress. Honest self-examination under adversarial pressure produced genuine partition.

Lock-Pressure Formula Gap

The reflexive application exposed a methodological limitation the binder didn't anticipate: the formula measures salience, not directional opposition, when advocates share underlying agreement. A genuine finding about domain-specificity of governance math.

Experimental: Invariant Weight Method
Exploratory Experiment
Verdict: Partially useful. Qualitative signal meaningful; quantitative precision is theater.
Works: Convergence detection (cache cluster identified reliably), contest detection (INV-25 directional disagreement visible), release-pattern telemetry (MINE's narrowing from 9.1 -> 6.6 visible across phases).
Fails: Lock-pressure values uniformly low. Formula designed for opposing tensions, but advocates here share underlying agreement. Stance-load totals (12.4 vs 12.8) within scoring noise — scope of claim, not quality of argument.
Next experiment: Separate relevance scoring (salience map) from alignment scoring (directional map). Test against a genuinely contested arena where advocates disagree on value, not just method.
